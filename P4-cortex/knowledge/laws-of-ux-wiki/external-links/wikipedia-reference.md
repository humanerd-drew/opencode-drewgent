## Cognitive Psychology

### [Aesthetic–usability effect](https: //en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aesthetic%E2%80%93usability_effect)

**Definition: ** People perceive more aesthetic designs as much more intuitive than less aesthetically pleasing ones.

**Key Points: **
- First studied by Masaaki Kurosu and Kaori Kashimura at Hitachi (1995)
- Aesthetic-usability bias: attractive = usable perception
- Cultural differences in aesthetic preferences
- Affects perceived value and performance

**Research Findings: **
- Cognitive style influences interaction (imagers vs verbalizers)
- Visual appeal improves perceived value (ERP study, 2021)
- Beautiful prototypes rated as more usable even when functionally identical

### [Fitts's law](https: //en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fitts%27s_law)

**Definition: ** Time to acquire a target = function of distance/size ratio.

**Formula: ** MT = a + b × log₂(2D/W)

**Key Concepts: **
- Index of Difficulty (ID) measured in bits
- Index of Performance (IP) = throughput (bits/sec)
- 2-component model: fast initial movement + slow precise adjustment

**UI Applications: **
- Big targets reduce error rates
- Screen edges = infinite targets (no overshoot)
- Magic corners (4 screen corners)
- Pie menus: equal distance to all options
