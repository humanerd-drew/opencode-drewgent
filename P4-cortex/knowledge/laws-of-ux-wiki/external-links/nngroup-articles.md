## Gestalt & Visual Perception

### [Proximity Principle in Visual Design](https: //www.nngroup.com/articles/gestalt-proximity/)
**Aurora Harley** | 2020-08-02

Design elements near each other are perceived as related, while elements spaced apart are perceived as belonging to separate groups.

**Key Points: **
- Items close together are likely to be perceived as part of the same group
- Whitespace separates groups effectively
- Proximity can overpower similarity cues like color or shape
- In responsive designs, proximity relationships may shift with screen size

**UI Applications: **
- Forms: Group related fields together with less spacing between label/input pairs
- Navigation: Separate utility nav from main nav using whitespace
- Content: Paragraph spacing creates clear groupings

---

### [Similarity Principle in Visual Design](https: //www.nngroup.com/articles/gestalt-similarity/)
**Aurora Harley** | 2020-09-06

Items which share a visual characteristic are perceived as more related than items that are dissimilar.

**Key Points: **
- Shared color creates powerful grouping signals
- Shape consistency helps users understand functionality
- Size indicates importance level
- Similarity works across distributed elements

**UI Applications: **
- Use consistent link colors (only for clickable elements)
- Same-color buttons = same importance level
- Navigation icons should share consistent style
